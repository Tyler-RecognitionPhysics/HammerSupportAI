Where Should My Team Respond to Customers?

 Short Answer
 Your dealership can respond to customers either:
 ✅ Inside the Hammer dashboard or ✅ Directly within your CRM [through the quick-click conversation link]
 Hammer supports both workflows, allowing your team to continue working leads using the process that fits your dealership best.

 How Customer Conversations Work
 When a customer submits a lead or sends a message, HammerAI begins engaging automatically using SMS or supported chat channels.
 As the conversation progresses, Hammer sends updates that may appear:

 As Required Actions inside Hammer

 As updates or merged activity within your CRM

 Both locations reference the same customer conversation.

 Recommended Best Practice
 Regardless of where your team prefers to work, we recommend opening the Hammer conversation link when responding to customers.
 This link may be available through:

 Email notifications

 CRM activity notes or comments

 Conversation summaries sent by Hammer

 Opening the conversation link allows your team to view the full interaction and respond from the same tracking number used by Hammer.

 Why Opening the Conversation Link Matters
 Responding through the conversation ensures:
 ✅ Messages come from the same dealership phone number ✅ Customers experience a consistent conversation ✅ AI and salesperson responses remain aligned ✅ Duplicate or overlapping messaging is avoided
 This helps prevent confusion for both your team and the customer.

 Taking Over a Conversation
 If a salesperson plans to handle communication directly, they can:

 Open the conversation link

 Reply to the customer from the same number

 Disable AI responses if desired

 Disabling AI ensures Hammer does not continue automated follow-up while your team is actively managing the conversation. [ Please note: If a new inbound lead from a provider is sent the AI would be re-enabled to handle that new inquiry]

 Working Fully Inside Your CRM
 Many dealerships prefer managing leads entirely within their CRM.
 When CRM deduplication merges Hammer updates successfully:

 The original salesperson remains assigned

 Customer ownership stays intact

 Conversations can be worked using existing dealership CRM workflows & the conversation link

 Hammer continues supporting engagement in the background while your CRM remains the primary workspace.

 Working Inside Hammer
 Dealerships may also respond directly using Required Actions inside Hammer to:

 See customers needing attention

 Open conversations quickly

 Text customers immediately

 Manage AI takeover settings

 Both workflows access the same customer engagement.

 Important to Know
 Hammer continuing a conversation while a salesperson responds separately can create mixed messaging.
 Opening the Hammer conversation link or disabling AI before responding helps maintain a clear and consistent customer experience.

 Best Practice
 Hammer manages engagement. Your team manages relationships.
 Use whichever system your dealership prefers — ensure responses occur within the shared conversation whenever possible.

 Need Help?
 If you're unsure where a conversation originated or how to take over messaging, Hammer Support can review an example conversation with you.
