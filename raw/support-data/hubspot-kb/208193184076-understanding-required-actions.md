Understanding Required Actions

 What Are Required Actions?
 Required Actions help your dealership know exactly when and where to step into a customer conversation .
 As HammerAI communicates with customers, it continuously evaluates the conversation and assigns Required Actions when dealership involvement is recommended.
 Think of Required Actions as your team’s daily follow-up list .

 How Required Actions Work
 When a customer submits a lead or sends a message:

 Hammer receives the inquiry

 Hammer begins the conversation automatically

 Hammer gathers information and engages the customer

 A Required Action is created when human follow-up is helpful or needed

 Your team can open the Required Action to immediately continue the conversation.

 Where to Find Required Actions
 Required Actions appear inside your Prospects view inside Hammer. For most CRMs, they can be found in the comments section of the lead update. [Typically found as the merged duplicate lead on the customer profile]
 Each Required Action indicates:

 The customer needing attention

 The recommended next step

 Direct access to the conversation

 Opening the prospect or conversation link allows your team to:

 View the full conversation

 Text the customer directly

 Disable AI responses if needed

 Continue the sales process

 Common Types of Required Actions
 You may see actions such as:

 Schedule appointment

 Call prospect

 Confirm appointment

 Provide vehicle information

 Help with financing

 These actions help prioritize follow-up so opportunities are not missed.

 Why Required Actions May Change or Update
 Hammer continues working conversations even after an action appears.
 Because of this, Required Actions may:

 Update

 Reappear

 Be replaced with a new action

 This is normal and means the customer is still actively engaging.
 Hammer is providing updated guidance based on the latest conversation activity.

 Why You May See Multiple Actions for One Customer
 Hammer sends conversation updates  as it progresses.
 If a customer continues responding or new information is gathered, additional Required Actions may appear.
 This does not mean duplicate outreach occurred.
 It ensures your team always knows the next best step.

 Required Actions vs Notifications
 These serve different purposes:
 ✅ Required Actions
 Tell your team what to do next .
 They guide workflow and follow-up.

 🔔 Notifications
 Alert users that activity occurred, such as:

 New conversation started

 Follow-up update

 Phone call received

 Notifications inform you and are optional . Required Actions guide you and are required – with some limited filters.

 What Happens If No Action Is Taken?
 Hammer will continue automated follow-up when appropriate.
 However, Required Actions indicate moments where dealership involvement typically improves outcomes, such as:

 Scheduling appointments

 Answering detailed questions

 Closing opportunities

 Timely follow-up helps maximize lead conversion.

 Best Practice in Hammer
 If using the Hammer Dashboard to work out of: We recommend reviewing Required Actions throughout the day and treating them as your dealership’s active opportunity queue.
 Many dealerships use Required Actions as their primary daily follow-up workflow.

 Need Help?
 If something looks unexpected, Support can review the conversation history quickly.
 Providing the customer name or conversation link helps us investigate faster.
