Hammer’s follow-up strategy is designed to maximize conversion by dynamically adjusting outreach based on *lead responsiveness*. The system is built to *capitalize quickly on high-intent leads*, while *maintaining pressure on colder leads* without being overbearing. Here’s a detailed breakdown of the follow-up strategy for both *unresponsive* and *very responsive* leads:

*Unresponsive to initial message*: follows up 2 times.

*Responsive:* Up to 3 times a week - sometimes back to back days, sometimes spread out. Depends on sentiment/context.

*Organically:* If a customer states for example, "I"m out of town. I'll check back next week." We will follow up around that time next week based on that context.

 *Carrier Considerations That Affect Frequency*
1. *Throughput Limits & Volume Caps*
• Carriers throttle how many messages can be sent per second/hour, especially from *application-to-person (A2P)* platforms like Hammer.
• *10DLC registration (dedicated local numbers)* helps boost deliverability, but frequency still matters to avoid flagging.
2. *Filtering Based on Message Patterns*
• If a lead receives *multiple messages per day* that seem automated and don't get replies, carriers may *flag or block* future messages.
• Carriers monitor *content similarity, timing, and user behavior (like not replying or marking as spam).*
3. *Unwanted Message Reports*
• If recipients *report* or *block* numbers frequently, it damages Hammer’s sender reputation, especially on shared or pooled numbers.
• This reinforces the need for *smart throttling and content variation*. (which we do)
